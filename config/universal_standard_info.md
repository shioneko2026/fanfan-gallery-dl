# Why Universal Standard?

You're building a personal archive from Fanbox, Fantia, and other platforms. Most people dump files into folders and call it done — until six months later when they can't find anything, can't tell what's from where, or accidentally re-download content they already have.

The Universal Standard naming pattern solves all of that automatically.

## You'll never lose track of what you have.

Every file carries its full identity: artist, date, title, platform, and post ID. Move files between drives, reorganize your folders, send them to a friend — the filename alone tells you everything. No spreadsheet needed. No memory required.

## Your folders sort themselves.

`YYYY-MM-DD` date format means your OS sorts files chronologically without any effort. Open a folder and you're looking at a clean timeline of an artist's work. Find the latest content instantly. Spot gaps at a glance.

## You'll never accidentally re-download.

Because the naming is deterministic — same content always produces the same filename — your download tool can skip files that already exist. No duplicates eating your disk space. No wasted bandwidth.

## You can always trace back to the source.

The post ID (`P12345678`) embedded in every filename is a direct link back to the original post. Need the description? Want to check if there were more files? Need to verify you got everything? The ID takes you straight there.

## You'll know exactly what's missing.

When an artist has 47 posts and you have 44 files, the dates and post IDs tell you precisely which three you're missing. Recovery becomes surgical instead of "just re-download everything."

## Community compatibility.

This is where Universal Standard pays off the most. When everyone names files the same way, the entire community benefits:

- Someone shares files on a forum? You can tell at a glance whether you already have them — the filenames match exactly.
- Receive a batch from a friend? Drop them into your folder. No renaming, no conflicts. Everything slots into place.
- Comparing collections? Dates and post IDs make it trivial to spot what one person has that another doesn't.

Community compatibility isn't about forcing everyone into a box — it's about removing the friction that makes sharing and organizing painful. When your naming pattern matches everyone else's, your archive stops being an island.
